Overview

PRISMS-MP is a powerful, massively parallel finite element code for conducting microstructural evolution simulations with a coupled phase field / crystal-plasticity model .

The guiding principles of PRISMS-MP are:

  • Providing a simple interface for solving a wide variety of partial differential equations (PDEs)
  • Parallel performance that meets or exceeds that of other phase field codes
  • Being extensible to allow users to add their own PDEs and modules
  • Providing a rich set of pre-built applications for common phase-field/CPFE coupled problems
  • benchmark code against other codes to ensure correctness
  • Being open source and freely available to the community

This code is developed by the PRedictive Integrated Structural Materials Science (PRISMS) Center at University of Michigan which is supported by the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE), Office of Basic Energy Sciences, Division of Materials Sciences and Engineering under Award DE-SC0008637.
